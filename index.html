Safe AI Practices

Understanding safe, responsible, and secure AI use

Knowledge & Guidance

Prompt Effectively

Clear Purpose

State exactly what you want AI to do or produce so the response is focused and relevant.

Provide Context

Give AI relevant background on your audience and constraints so it responds appropriately.

Reasoning or Sources

Ask AI to explain its approach or cite sources so you can understand, verify and trust the response.

Transparency and Trust

Use AI to explore information and options, not to make final decisions or judgments for you.

How AI Fails, Why It Matters, and How to Use It Responsibly

AI tools like Microsoft Copilot Chat can support brainstorming, drafting, and summarizing. But AI does not understand meaning or truth the way people do. It identifies patterns in data, which means it may sometimes produce confident and polished answers that are incorrect.

1. AI can produce inaccurate information:

AI does not evaluate whether information is true, current, or credible. Instead, it predicts what text is most likely to come next based on patterns in existing data.

What to do: Ask the AI to provide citations or references when appropriate. Review the sources and verify the information using trusted resources.

2. AI can reflect bias or generate harmful content:

AI systems are trained on large amounts of publicly available data, including content from the internet. Because that data can reflect societal biases, AI generated outputs may unintentionally repeat or amplify them.

What to do: All AI generated content should be reviewed for inclusivity, and tone.

3. Not all AI tools protect privacy:

Publicly available AI tools may not meet expectations of confidentiality. In some cases, user inputs may be stored or used to improve the tool over time.

What to do: Use approved workplace AI tools such as Microsoft Copilot for work related tasks, as they are designed to protect privacy and confidentiality. When using other AI tools for personal purposes, avoid entering personal, sensitive, or confidential information.

The bottom line

AI can save time and support creativity, but it doesn’t replace human judgment. People remain responsible for checking accuracy, identifying bias, protecting privacy, and deciding what is appropriate to share.
